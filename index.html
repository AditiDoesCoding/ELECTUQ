Election Glossary

Master the language of democracy.

Democracy 101

The Democratic Pulse

India's election calendar never stops. Understand the cycles that power the world's largest democracy.

🇮🇳

General Elections
(Lok Sabha)

Held every 5 years to elect the Central Government. You vote for your Member of Parliament (MP).

Next: Summer 2029
🏛️

Assembly Elections
(Vidhan Sabha)

Held every 5 years per state. You vote for your MLA to form the State Government. These happen at different times across India!

Cyclical by State
🏡

Local Body Polls
(Panchayat/Civic)

The grassroots level. Electing Sarpanchs, Councilors, and Mayors for your immediate neighborhood and village.

Managed by State EC
⚡

Bye-Elections
(The 'Fillers')

The emergency polls. If a seat becomes vacant (due to resignation or death), a Bye-election is held within 6 months to fill that specific seat.

Happens Year-Round

Why the 5-Year Cycle?

The Constitution of India mandates that the Lok Sabha and State Assemblies have a fixed term of 5 years. This ensures regular accountability where the government must return to the people for a fresh mandate.

Accountability 🤝
Fresh Mandate 🗳️

Did You Know?

You can be registered in only one constituency at a time. Regardless of whether it's a General or Assembly election, voting twice in different places is a punishable offense!
